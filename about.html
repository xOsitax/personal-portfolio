About Me

My Hobbies

Programming

I went to a vocational highschool, I took a Computer System Technology (CST) class, instead of the usual Art or Theater classes. In that class I learned all about the field of Information Technology (IT). I gained the skills to build computers and troubleshoot computer issues, as well as, set up networks and maintain them.

In my junior year, in that CST class, I learned about the Python programming language. Immediately, I was hooked with being able to write programs and see them run. From that moment on I decided that I wanted to learn more, which took me down the path of Computer Science.

Soccer

In Peru, two sports dominated over everything else: Soccer and Volleyball. At the age of 4 with the encouragement of my father, I began playing soccer for a small team in a minor league meant for children ages 4-7. My father was a fanatic of soccer, so it was only natural that he would want me to play as well.

Thanks to my father, soccer became a big part of who I am. Whenever the weather is good enough you can be sure that I'll be at the soccer field. Soccer not only makes me happy, it also helps me stay in shape, especially with the amount of food I can eat.

Languages & Cultures

My father would tell me stories of the places he had travel to during his 20s. In particular, the United States and Japan were two places he had lived in for years. Through his tales, I started to become curious about foreign countries and their cultures.

Thanks to my father, I feel in love with learning new languages and learning about the lifestyles that people would lead in those countries. If I ever have the opportunity, there are 3 places I would want to live in for a couple of years: Japan, South Korea, and Germany.
